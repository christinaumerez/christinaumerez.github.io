8 Reasons You Should Be Using Google Search Ads

by Christina Umerez
A Quick Recap

Google is the top used search engine currently on the market, in fact it receives over 63,000 searches per second. With this astronomical amount of daily searches, a company would be crazy to not want to reach these available audiences.

However, ranking organically for a top spot in these searches would require extensive website work to build up an SEO strategy that would compete with the top players of every business vertical. And that’s where the benefits of Google Ads come in to play.

Google Ads is a pay per click advertising channel that reaches people through the Google Search Network (and Google Display network- but we’ll discuss that another time). It allows smaller businesses to takeover the #1 spot from large corporations and receive the attention they deserve. Local restaurants can outrank giant franchises. A craft brewery can appear above largely distributed brewers. We can thank Googles prioritization of quality and relevancy for this.

While duh- Google is in it for the money- Ad ranks are determined by a combination of quality score AND bid price. We’ll discuss these two factors shortly! These ad ranks are important as they are what go through the lightning speed unseen auction behind each search. This constant auction factors in your campaign settings, keywords and ad rank to determine who appears for a search. Understanding the nuances of how this auction works is important to outranking your competitors and reaching your target audiences.

1. Googles Unparalleled Reach

Google search is exceptional compared to anything else you’ll find online. As we previously mentioned, it receives over 63,000 searches per second! The brand name has morphed into its own verb. Don’t believe me? Google it.

Google It.

Good luck finding any other online resource that can reach audiences in mass amounts that could ever compete with Google. Google spans further than just it’s search engine. It hosts gmail, YouTube, 2 million + websites, and over 650,000 apps. That’s enough coverage to reach 90% of world wide internet users!

2. Targeting Capabilities

Speaking of target audiences, Google gains knowledge about a person's wants and needs everytime they create a search or visit a website. It creates affinity audiences and in-market audiences that allow us as advertisers to target those most likely to purchase our offerings.

Through Google Ads we can both target audiences through keyword targeting, combined with demographic targeting.

Keyword targeting - Reaching people who search for your keywords, meaning they are already looking for what you have to offer

Demographic targeting - Gender, Age, Online interests are some of the ways we can segment who sees your ads.

Google Ads can quickly narrow down your audience to ensure your ad money is only going towards those most likely to convert.

3. Scalable

Finding strategies that are scalable are every marketers challenge. Google Ads is one of the few solutions that scale ideally with the spend of a campaign. Doubling your budget does not require double the work. Your traffic and results are reflective of your budget making it an ideal channel that grow as you do.

4. Clear success metrics

Print, TV, magazine and other traditional marketing channels, while are successful, can not present the clear metrics that PPC ads can. Some of the most common and important metrics measurable include:
- Impressions
- Cost
- Clicks
- CPC
- Conversions
- Impressions
- CTR
- Cost per conversion

Digital services provide these accurate insights unparalleled by traditional strategies. This available data allows you to make informed decisions, measure your success and allocate costs based on what is working.

5. Quick results

SEO and PPC are the two prongs of search engine marketing. While SEO should never be ignored when it comes to growing your traffic, it is very front loaded, requiring much time to return the results you need to get your business going. To rank for keywords you need well written articles, back-links and a well planned out website to build Google trust. PPC (Google Ads) gives you instant visibility to supplement your SEO efforts.

Well planned out Google Ads can drive the traffic you need in a fraction of the time it would take to generate the same SEO results.

6. Compete with the big dogs

Big money buys big advertising, so how do you become top of mind to your consumers against the largest corporations? Google Ads allow you to fairly compete against your competitors to appear to a shared target market. By taking in multiple factors such as location, keywords, bid price,...

7. Pay Per Click

Most advertising channels are great at bringing awareness to a brand, achieving what we call a large reach. While for some companies, this can be an extremely useful way to grow, it does not accurately measure whether a strategy actually resulted in engagement from a viewer.

Google Ads rely on a revenue model in which the advertiser only pays when a consumer interacts with the brand. Exposure is still guaranteed similar to a reach campaign, however, we are only charged when the campaign succeeds at driving traffic. This revenue model ensures we are not wasting money on tactics that don’t work.

8. Retarget warm audiences

Those familiar with your brand and have previously engaged with it are called warm audiences. It is common that people click into your website through your Google Search Ad, and then get swept away in their busy life never to think of your brand again. Luckily, Google is a powerful tool that allows us to identify these warm audiences. People who are already warm are more likely to re-click an advertisement they see and be convinced to buy. To further your Google Search ads, you can ad Display ads within the Google Ads platform to re-engage these lost website visitors.

Paid advertisements offer several benefits that will help you and your business reach your business goals whether it be reach, traffic, conversions and more.
